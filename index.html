Available Minerals

Available minerals listed below are currently at my disposal.
This means that I am able to use them right away for crafting jewellery products. It is of course possible to use different materials, but this will extend your order time due to shipping of the material.
If you have any question about materials/minerals you want to use for your order please contact me.

Sapphire

Sapphire is the most precious and valuable blue gemstone. It is a very desirable gemstone due to its excellent color, hardness, durability, and luster.

Diamond

Diamond is named from the Greek "adamas" meaning unconquerable as it is the hardest natural substance on Earth.

​Emerald

​Emerald, the green variety of Beryl. Its green color, durability and rarity, make it one of the most expensive gemstones. Deep green is the most desired color in Emeralds. (i general the lighter the color of an Emerald, the lesser its value.

​Opal

Opal is one of the most colorful gems - very fine examples can even be more valuable than Diamond. The iridescent color that appeares on the surface is often called opalescence. The intensity and distribution of the color flashes is a determining factor in the value of an Opal.

​Rose Quartz

​​Rose Quartz is the rosy pink variety of Quartz. Its color is usually soft, ranging from very light pink to medium pink in intensity. Rose Quartz is fairly common and found in many places throughout the world, often in large crystals.

​​Ruby

Ruby is one of the most well known gemstones and is famous for its bright red color. It is also very hard and durable with a shiny lustre. Transparent versions are rare, very pure examples can be more rare than diamonds.
